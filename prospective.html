Areas of Interest for Prospective Graduate Students

Join ACES Lab and contribute to transformational research at the intersection of power systems, AI, and cybersecurity.

ACES selects a few students to join our research group each year. We are starting several new exciting projects in the research areas listed below. If you are a top student, are interested in these areas, and meet the profile descriptions, we invite you to apply to our Ph.D. program in the School of Electrical and Computer Engineering.

1

Cyber-Physical Security

Ideal Profile:
  • Cyber-security, information security, network security
  • Cyber-security of industrial control systems
  • Machine learning applications to cyber-security
  • Familiarity with critical infrastructure systems such as the electricity grid
2

Electricity System Decentralized, Stochastic Optimization

Ideal Profile:
  • Deep understanding of Optimal Power Flow and Unit Commitment
  • Decentralized Optimization
  • Stochastic Optimization
  • Expertise in large-scale computation
3

Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning in Energy Systems

Ideal Profile:
  • Strong background in Machine Learning, Reinforcement Learning, LLMs
  • Experience with advanced predictive models
  • Experience with ML applications to energy systems
  • Strong software skills
4

Decentralized Power Electronics Control

Ideal Profile:
  • Solid background in power system operations
  • Solid background in power system stability
  • Solid background in power electronics and DER controls
  • Decentralized and networked control
5

Energy Robotics

Ideal Profile:
  • Solid background in Robotics
  • Pattern Recognition, Image processing, Control Systems
  • Familiarity with mobile power applications
  • Familiarity with electric grids or transportation systems
